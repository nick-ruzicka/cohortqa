### 1. No 'Back to list' on /add.html _(missing_action)_ · 🔀 cross-persona
**Severity:** high → high  ·  **Effort:** S  ·  **Personas affected:** cautious-first-timer, error-prone, keyboard-only, returning-user, rusher, skeptic, skimmer, wanderer

The add form has no back/cancel affordance. Every persona — cautious, rusher, keyboard-only, skimmer — was trapped on the form with no escape except the browser back button (which risks losing draft state).

**Proposed fix:** Add a visible 'Back to list' link (and/or a 'Cancel' button next to Save) at the top-left of /add.html, linking to /index.html. Make it keyboard-focusable and high-contrast so visual-first skimmers can spot it in <5s.

**Implementation approach:** Edit examples/quickstart/app/add.html: insert <a id="back-link" href="index.html">← Back to list</a> in a header region above the form. Mirror the markup in detail.html and done.html for consistency. No JS state change needed.

**Cross-persona signature:** all 8 personas hit — universal friction, not persona-specific

**Evidence:**
- cautious-first-timer: 'selector probe for back_to_list returned matched_count=0'
- rusher: 'no back link found on /add.html; persona is trapped'
- keyboard-only: 'cannot navigate away from the form without submitting'
- skeptic: 'save persists to db without preview AND no cancel path'

### 2. No 'Back to list' on /detail.html _(missing_action)_ · 🔀 cross-persona
**Severity:** high → high  ·  **Effort:** S  ·  **Personas affected:** cautious-first-timer, error-prone, keyboard-only, returning-user, rusher, skeptic, skimmer, wanderer

The detail view exposes Mark Done and Delete but no way back to the list. Combined with destructive actions, this traps every persona and is especially dangerous for cautious/error-prone users who may click destructive buttons just to escape.

**Proposed fix:** Add a visible 'Back to list' link at the top-left of /detail.html, positioned BEFORE (above and left of) the Mark Done and Delete buttons so escape is the first affordance seen.

**Implementation approach:** Edit examples/quickstart/app/detail.html: insert <a id="back-link" href="index.html">← Back to list</a> above the action button row. Optionally add aria-label for screen readers.

**Cross-persona signature:** all 8 personas hit — universal friction, not persona-specific

**Evidence:**
- returning-user: 'muscle memory from prior use' expected back link
- skimmer: 'visual-first user trapped, no prominent affordance'
- cautious-first-timer: 'irreversible actions without escape route'
- wanderer: 'breaks exploration mental model'

### 3. /done.html is a navigation dead-end _(empty_state)_ · 🔀 cross-persona
**Severity:** high → high  ·  **Effort:** M  ·  **Personas affected:** cautious-first-timer, error-prone, keyboard-only, returning-user, skeptic, wanderer

The done list renders 58 chars of text with zero visible actions, no 'Back to active' link, and no 'Restore' affordance per item. Six personas explicitly reached this dead-end; the remaining two (rusher, skimmer) did not navigate there but would hit the same wall.

**Proposed fix:** On /done.html, add: (1) a 'Back to active' link at the top, (2) a 'Restore' button next to each completed todo that moves it back to active, and (3) an empty-state message with a CTA when there are no completed items.

**Implementation approach:** Edit examples/quickstart/app/done.html: add the back link in the header (mirroring add/detail), render each completed item with a <button class="restore-todo">Restore</button>, and wire a restore handler that flips the done flag in localStorage/db. Add an empty-state <p> with a link back to /index.html when the list is empty.

**Cross-persona signature:** 6 of 8 personas hit — the 2 that didn't (rusher, skimmer) simply never navigated to /done.html in their fast sessions

**Evidence:**
- cautious-first-timer: 'stranded in a read-only dead-end'
- keyboard-only: 'zero visible actions, no back or restore'
- skeptic: 'no recovery path'
- wanderer: 'sparse, action-less dead-end'

### 4. Priority chip on detail has no legend _(scoring_opacity)_ · medium-confidence · 🔀 cross-persona
**Severity:** medium → medium  ·  **Effort:** S  ·  **Personas affected:** cautious-first-timer, error-prone

The detail view shows a priority chip but no legend or tooltip explaining what each level means or how it was assigned. Cautious and error-prone personas wanted to understand the indicator before acting.

**Proposed fix:** Add a small legend or hover tooltip near the priority chip on /detail.html explaining the priority scale (e.g., Low/Medium/High and what each implies).

**Implementation approach:** In examples/quickstart/app/detail.html, wrap the priority chip in a <span title="..."> with a plain-English explanation, or render a small inline legend below it. No data-model change needed.

**Cross-persona signature:** 2 of 8 personas hit — both deliberative readers (cautious + error-prone); fast personas (rusher, skimmer) didn't notice

**Evidence:**
- cautious-first-timer: 'will want to understand what each priority level means before acting'
- error-prone: 'priority chip but no legend or explanation'

### 5. Save commits to DB with no preview or confirmation _(missing_action)_ · medium-confidence · 🔀 cross-persona
**Severity:** medium → medium  ·  **Effort:** M  ·  **Personas affected:** skeptic, error-prone

The Save action on /add.html persists immediately to the database with no preview step or confirmation, and no draft persistence if the user navigates away. Privacy-conscious and error-prone personas want a chance to verify before committing.

**Proposed fix:** Either (a) add a confirmation step / preview before commit, or (b) persist the draft to localStorage on field change so accidental navigation doesn't lose work. Option (b) is lighter weight.

**Implementation approach:** In examples/quickstart/app/add.html, attach 'input' listeners on title/description fields that write to localStorage under a 'draft_todo' key; on page load, hydrate fields from that key; clear it on successful save. Option (a) requires a confirmation modal — heavier.

**Cross-persona signature:** 2 of 8 personas hit — both trust/safety-oriented personas; speed-oriented personas would find a confirm step annoying, so the localStorage-draft variant is the safer fix

**Evidence:**
- skeptic: 'save persists to db.todos without preview or confirmation'
- error-prone: 'form does not persist across navigation events'
